## Working topic
How the Pacific Walker Circulation responds to forced (anthropogenic and volcanic) versus internal drivers in the isotope-enabled Community Earth System Model (iCESM) Last Millennium Ensemble, and what oxygen-isotope (δ¹⁸O) fingerprint of precipitation can distinguish strengthening from weakening regimes.

## Queries
- What is the observational and model evidence on whether the Pacific Walker Circulation has strengthened or weakened under late-20th-century and early-21st-century anthropogenic forcing?
- How does precipitation δ¹⁸O vary with Pacific Walker Circulation strength in isotope-enabled GCMs, and can it serve as a diagnostic fingerprint distinguishing circulation regimes?
- What do recent analyses of CESM Last Millennium Ensemble output reveal about tropical Pacific SST-SLP covariability under combined volcanic + solar + greenhouse forcing?
- How do paleoclimate proxy reconstructions from the iso2k database constrain past Pacific Walker Circulation variability over the last millennium?
- Does the simulated response of tropical Pacific zonal SST gradients to radiative forcing reconcile the apparent discrepancy between model-predicted weakening and observed strengthening of the Walker cell?

## Filters
- **Year range:** 2020–present
- **Max results per query:** 15
- **Include retracted content:** no

Preferred journals: *Journal of Climate*, *Nature*, *Nature Climate Change*, *Geophysical Research Letters*, *Climate Dynamics*. Track authors: Karnauskas, Sobel, Coats, Stevenson, Brierley, DiNezio. Emphasize work using isotope-enabled GCMs or proxy-system modeling.
